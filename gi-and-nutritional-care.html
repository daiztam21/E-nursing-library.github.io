Gastrointestinal and Nutritional Care

This section focuses on digestive health and nutritional support. Proper gastrointestinal and nutritional care is essential for maintaining digestive health and ensuring adequate nutritional support for patients who are unable to meet their dietary needs through oral intake. The gastrointestinal system plays a crucial role in digestion, nutrient absorption, and waste elimination, and any disruption in its function can lead to complications such as malnutrition, dehydration, and electrolyte imbalances. Providing appropriate interventions, including enteral and parenteral nutrition, bowel management, and dietary modifications, helps prevent complications and supports overall recovery. Ensuring adequate nutrition is vital for wound healing, immune function, and overall patient well-being. Additionally, patient education on dietary choices and gastrointestinal care promotes long-term health and enhances quality of life. By implementing proper gastrointestinal and nutritional care strategies, healthcare providers can improve patient outcomes and prevent complications associated with inadequate nutrition and digestive disorders.

Nasogastric (NG) Tube Insertion & Feeding

A Nasogastric Tube Insertion

Purpose:

  • To decompress the stomach in cases of obstruction or ileus.
  • To administer enteral nutrition and medications.
  • To remove gastric contents for diagnostic or therapeutic purposes.

Procedure:

  1. Verify the physician’s order and gather supplies.
  2. Explain the procedure to the patient and ensure consent.
  3. Position the patient in high Fowler’s position.
  4. Measure the tube from the nose to the earlobe to the xiphoid process.
  5. Lubricate the tip of the tube with water-soluble lubricant.
  6. Insert the tube through the nostril, directing it downward and backward.
  7. Encourage swallowing to assist passage into the esophagus.
  8. Verify placement by aspirating gastric contents and confirming with pH testing.
  9. Secure the tube with tape and attach to the appropriate suction or feeding system.
  10. Document the procedure and monitor for complications.

B. NG Tube Feeding

Purpose:

- To provide nutrition to patients unable to consume food orally.

Procedure:

  1. Verify the physician’s order and assess tube placement.
  2. Check residual volume before feeding.
  3. Administer feeding at room temperature using a gravity method or feeding pump.
  4. Flush the tube with sterile water before and after feeding.
  5. Monitor for signs of aspiration, nausea, or discomfort.
  6. Keep the patient’s head elevated for at least 30-60 minutes post-feeding.
  7. Document intake, tolerance, and any complications.

Enteral Nutrition & Tube Feeding Management

Purpose:

- To provide nutritional support through a feeding tube when oral intake is inadequate.

Procedure:

  1. Ensure proper tube placement before initiating feeding.
  2. Use sterile technique when handling feeding formulas.
  3. Administer feedings slowly to prevent intolerance.
  4. Monitor blood glucose levels in diabetic patients.
  5. Rotate feeding sites if using a PEG or jejunostomy tube.
  6. Prevent clogging by flushing tubes regularly with sterile water.
  7. Maintain hygiene and inspect for signs of infection or irritation.
  8. Educate caregivers on proper feeding techniques and troubleshootin

Parenteral Nutrition (TPN & PPN)

Purpose:

- To provide complete or partial nutrition intravenously when the gastrointestinal tract is not functional.

Procedure:

  1. Verify the physician’s order and assess the need for parenteral nutrition.
  2. Prepare the patient and obtain central or peripheral venous access.
  3. Use strict aseptic technique when handling the solution and equipment.
  4. Monitor infusion rate and adjust as needed based on patient response.
  5. Regularly check blood glucose, electrolytes, and hydration status.
  6. Observe for signs of infection, thrombosis, or metabolic imbalances.
  7. Gradually taper TPN to prevent rebound hypoglycemia.
  8. Document findings and patient response.

Stoma Care & Ostomy Management

Purpose:

- To maintain hygiene, prevent infection, and ensure proper function of the stoma.

Procedure:

  1. Assess stoma for color, swelling, and signs of infection.
  2. Cleanse stoma and surrounding skin with warm water.
  3. Ensure a proper fit of the ostomy appliance to prevent leakage.
  4. Educate patients on dietary modifications to reduce gas and odor.
  5. Monitor for complications such as prolapse, necrosis, or stenosis.
  6. Encourage self-care and provide emotional support.

Bowel Training & Constipation Management

Purpose:

- To establish regular bowel movements and prevent constipation.

Procedure:

  1. Encourage a high-fiber diet and adequate hydration.
  2. Establish a consistent toileting schedule.
  3. Promote physical activity to enhance bowel motility.
  4. Use stool softeners or laxatives as prescribed.
  5. Monitor bowel movements and address any changes promptly.
  6. Educate patients on lifestyle modifications for long-term bowel health.

Enema Administration

Purpose:

- To relieve constipation or cleanse the bowel before a procedure.

Procedure:

  1. Verify the physician’s order and patient’s condition.
  2. Explain the procedure and ensure privacy.
  3. Position the patient in the left lateral Sims’ position.
  4. Lubricate the tip of the enema tubing and insert gently.
  5. Administer the enema solution slowly to prevent discomfort.
  6. Encourage the patient to retain the solution as long as possible.
  7. Assist the patient to the toilet or bedpan for evacuation.
  8. Monitor for any adverse reactions and document the procedure.

Gastric Lavage & Decontamination

Purpose:

- To remove toxic substances or blood from the stomach.

Procedure:

  1. Indicated for toxin ingestion, bleeding control, or decompression.
  2. Position the patient in a semi-Fowler’s or left lateral position.
  3. Insert a large-bore NG tube using aseptic technique.
  4. Lavage with warm saline or prescribed solution in small increments.
  5. Monitor for signs of aspiration or distress.
  6. Discontinue the procedure once return fluid is clear.
  7. Document findings and notify the physician of any concerns.

Dietary Modifications & Nutritional Counseling

A. Dietary Modifications

Purpose:

- To provide dietary adjustments tailored to the patient’s medical condition.

Procedure:

  1. Assess the patient’s dietary needs based on medical conditions.
  2. Implement therapeutic diets such as:
    • Low-sodium for hypertension.
    • Diabetic diet for glucose control.
    • Renal diet for kidney disease.
  3. Educate patients on food selection and preparation.
  4. Encourage portion control and balanced meals.
  5. Monitor weight, BMI, and lab values regularly.

B. Nutritional Counseling

Purpose:

- To educate and support patients in achieving proper nutrition and lifestyle habits.

Procedure:

  1. Identify potential nutritional deficiencies and address them.
  2. Provide education on proper meal planning.
  3. Support lifestyle changes for long-term nutritional health.
  4. Work with dietitians to develop personalized nutrition plans.
  5. Offer psychological support for patients with eating disorders.
  6. Encourage adherence through follow-ups and progress tracking.
