Diagnostic and Special Procedures

A. ECG/EKG Basics

  • Measures electrical activity of the heart to detect arrhythmias, ischemia, and other cardiac conditions.
  • Standard 12-lead ECG provides a comprehensive view of heart function.

B. ECG/EKG Procedure

  1. Gather equipment (ECG machine, electrodes, conductive gel).
  2. Explain the procedure to the patient.
  3. Position the patient in a supine position.
  4. Attach electrodes in the correct placement.
  5. Ensure no interference from external devices.
  6. Record the ECG tracing and interpret findings.

Blood Transfusion & Administration

A. Blood Transfusion Indications

Used to replace blood loss, treat anemia, or support critical patients.

B. Blood Transfusion Procedure

  1. Verify physician's order.
  2. Perform patient identification and informed consent.
  3. Crossmatch and check blood type compatibility.
  4. Prepare IV line with normal saline.
  5. Administer blood slowly, monitoring for reactions.
  6. Document transfusion details and patient response.

Specimen Collection & Handling

A. Types of Specimens

Blood, urine, stool, sputum, cerebrospinal fluid (CSF), wound cultures.

B. General Guidelines for Specimen Collection

  1. Verify patient identity and label specimens correctly.
  2. Use appropriate sterile techniques.
  3. Transport specimens in proper containers.
  4. Store at required temperatures until analysis.

Glucometer & Blood Sugar Monitoring

A. Purpose

To monitor blood glucose levels for diabetic patients or those with metabolic disorders.

B. Procedure

  1. Wash hands and wear gloves.
  2. Insert test strip into glucometer.
  3. Prick the patient's fingertip with a lancet.
  4. Place a blood drop on the test strip.
  5. Read and record the glucose level.
  6. Dispose of used materials properly.

Diagnostic Imaging Assistance

A. Common Imaging Procedures

X-ray, CT scan, MRI, ultrasound.

B. Nursing Responsibilities

  1. Prepare and educate the patient.
  2. Remove metal objects if required.
  3. Assist with positioning for optimal imaging.
  4. Monitor for contrast dye reactions if applicable.

Lumbar Puncture & Cerebrospinal Fluid (CSF) Collection

A. Purpose

To diagnose infections, bleeding, or neurological conditions.

B. Procedure

  1. Obtain patient consent.
  2. Position patient in lateral decubitus or sitting position.
  3. Cleanse the puncture site with antiseptic.
  4. Assist the physician in inserting the needle.
  5. Collect CSF in sterile tubes.
  6. Monitor patient for complications like headache or infection.

Intravenous Pyelogram (IVP) & Renal Scans

A. IVP Purpose

Evaluates kidneys, ureters, and bladder using contrast dye and X-rays.

B. Nursing Responsibilities

  1. Check for allergies to contrast dye.
  2. Ensure patient hydration before and after the procedure.
  3. Educate patient on possible side effects.
  4. Monitor for reactions and kidney function post-procedure.

Holter Monitoring & Cardiac Stress Testing

A. Holter Monitor

A portable ECG device worn for 24-48 hours to detect arrhythmias.

B. Nursing Role

  1. Explain the procedure and attach electrodes.
  2. Ensure the patient avoids water exposure.
  3. Instruct on keeping a symptom diary.
  4. Remove device and send data for analysis.

C. Cardiac Stress Test

Measures heart function under exercise or medication-induced stress.

D. Procedure

  1. Obtain baseline ECG and vitals.
  2. Monitor ECG while patient walks on a treadmill or receives medication.
  3. Observe for signs of distress or abnormalities.
  4. Stop test if complications arise and document findings.
